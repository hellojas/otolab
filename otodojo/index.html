what actually helps you figure out chords

The skill of hearing "oh, that's a ii–V–I" decomposes into three smaller skills, and they're best trained in this order:

  1. Bass first. The bass note is the loudest, most isolated pitch in almost every mix, and root motion is what defines a progression. Train it with the intervals drill (P4/P5/m3 up and down are 80% of real root motion), then the basslines drill, which is real songs reduced to exactly this skill.
  2. Quality second. Once you have the root, is what's on top major, minor, dominant, half-diminished? That's the qualities drill. Learn triads cold before sevenths.
  3. Function last — and it's the multiplier. Root + quality relative to the key is the roman numeral, and roman numerals are why you can hear a song once and play it in any key. The degrees drill trains hearing a chord as "the IV" rather than "an F". Then the song quiz puts it together on real tunes.

A good session: 5 minutes of intervals as a warm-up, 5 of degrees, then song quizzes until your ears tire. When a song beats you, don't reveal — replay at 0.5× and hunt the bass line degree by degree. And when you meet a song in the wild, grab its chords from a tab site and feed them to paste a tab — transcribe-then-quiz on anything.
