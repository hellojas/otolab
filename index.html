What actually helps you figure out chords. Naming a progression decomposes into three skills, best trained in order:

  1. Bass first. The bass is the most isolated pitch in nearly every mix, and root motion is the progression. Train it with intervals (P4/P5/m3 both ways cover most real root motion), then basslines — real songs reduced to exactly this skill.
  2. Quality second. Given the root, is the color major, minor, dominant, half-diminished? That's qualities. Triads cold before sevenths.
  3. Function last — the multiplier. Root + quality relative to the key is the roman numeral, and hearing "IV going iv" instead of "C going Cm" is what transfers between songs and keys. Train it with degrees, then apply it on real tunes with the song quiz and the standards section below.

A good session: 5 minutes of intervals to warm up, 5 of degrees, then song quizzes or a standards transcription until your ears tire. When a tune beats you, don't reveal — replay at 0.5× and hunt the bass line degree by degree. Met a song in the wild? Grab its chords from a tab site and feed paste a tab.
